My Teaching Philosophy

My Aspirations, Goals, and Objectives

As a B.Ed candidate, I will:

  • Create a classroom environment where students feel safe and supported, and where learning is an exciting and fun endeavor, not something they are incapable of or scared to try.
  • Develop student scientific literacy and confidence, encouraging them in their curiosity and helping them feel confident enough in their scientific skills to question and research information they encounter.
  • Incorporate a thinking classroom style and encourage inquiry-based learning, while still integrating Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and varied assessment.

For My Students, I Will:

  • Make learning fun and accessible, while maintaining high expectations and fostering each student's belief in their capability for high achievement.
  • Equip students with the scientific confidence to navigate an increasingly technological and scientifically dependent world.
  • Foster inquiry and curiosity, allowing students to practice learning through exploration, getting stuck, and asking questions through UDL principles while emphasizing a thinking-based environment.

Methods to Reach These Goals

To achieve these objectives, I will:

  • Emphasize student relationship building, always putting students first in my practice to tailor lessons to the learners in front of me rather than following a rigid schedule.
  • Infuse fun and engaging activities into science learning, constantly reworking and improving lessons to maintain excitement and curiosity.
  • Design and implement a thinking classroom curriculum that balances high expectations with student-centered approaches, ensuring lessons remain challenging and accessible.
